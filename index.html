In Elmhurst, a Commmunity Sculpture Garden Provides Relief to Park-Starved Residents

November 11, 2020

By Sara Herschander

In 2017, a group of local artists and volunteers established the Elmhurst Sculpture Garden on a small parcel of land in Elmhurst, Queens, the most overcrowded community in Queens.

During a pandemic in which parks have become a refuge for New Yorkers, in Queens Community District 4, which encompasses Elmhurst and Corona, every 1,000 residents share less than 1/5 of an acre of parkland. Neighboring Community District 3 shares a similar pattern of overcrowding and insufficient park space. Residents in these two districts have up to 40x less park access than residents of other districts.

"Elmhurst really needs a lot more green space. Other communities have gardens, parkland, and places set aside for recreation and relaxation," said James McMenamin, a lifelong Elmhurst resident and Vice President of the Newtown Civic Association. He called the 2,500 square foot Elmhurst Sculpture Garden a "nice little oasis" for the community.

Queens Community Districts 3 and 4 encompass the most overcrowded neighborhoods in Queens: Elmhurst, Jackson Heights, and Corona.

Residents of Elmhurst, Jackson Heights, and Corona receive up to 40 times less park space than residents of less crowded neighborhoods, like Bayside and Douglaston.
